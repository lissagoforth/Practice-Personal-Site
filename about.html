My Story

I was born and raised in Nashville, TN. I attended MTSU and graduated with a degree in Psychology with a minor in Sociology. I originally had the intention of going to graduate school for Behavioral Neuroscience. However, when faced with the question of what I wanted to research specifically I came up short. I decided to postpone grad school and focus on repaying the student loans I already had instead of racking up even more debt. Fast forward 5 years and I was still just working to pay bills and hadn't settled into a career. This was very uncomfortable for me. I had this persistent feeling of under-achievment. I began looking for any way to break the cycle. That is how I found my way to Nashville software School. Software development seemed to offer nearly an infinte number of career options. That alone was enough to convince me to start learning how to code. Plus, given the nature of technology, it seemed very likely to me that I would eventually be able to pursue neuroscience via programming nano technology that could be implemented within the human body. This all sounds very sci-fi but I truly believe this is where medicine is headed.
